Both Knee Replacement

In a simultaneous procedure both knees are replaced in one operation, on the same day under one anesthesia.  This means that there is one hospitalization and one recovery period.

Single Knee Replacement

Both knee replacements take place as two separate surgical events.

ACL Reconstruction

Anterior cruciate ligament reconstruction is a surgical tissue graft replacement of the anterior cruciate ligament, located in the knee, to restore its function after an injury.

Single Hip Replacement

staged bilateral total hip replacement, in which your hips are replaced in two separate surgeries..

Both Hip Replacement

Simultaneous bilateral total hip replacement, in which both hip joints are replaced in one surgery.

Shoulder Replacement

Shoulder replacement is a surgical procedure in which all or part of the glenohumeral joint is replaced by a prosthetic implant

Shoulder Rotator Cuff Repair

Surgery to repair a torn rotator cuff most often involves re-attaching the tendon to the head of humerus (upper arm bone).

Spine Fusion Surgery

Spinal fusion, also called spondylodesis or spondylosyndesis, is a neurosurgical orthopedic surgical technique that joins two or more vertebrae

Scoliosis Surgery

The operation for scoliosis is a spinal fusion. The basic idea is to realign and fuse together the curved vertebrae so that they heal into a single, solid bone.

Spine Tumor Surgery

Surgical removal of a spine tumor is indicated for patients who may benefit from the tumor removal, either in terms of removing the cancer and/or lessening any severe symptoms associated with the tumor

Spinal Decompression

Spinal decompression is a type of traction therapy applied to the spine in an attempt to bring about several theoretical benefits .

Minimally Invasive Spine Surgery

In order to treat spine problems with less injury to the muscles and other normal structures in the spine. In these procedures, doctors use specialized instruments to access the spine through small incisions

Artificial Spine Lumbar Disc Replacement

In artificial disk replacement, worn or damaged disk material between the small bones in the spine (vertebrae) is removed and replaced with a synthetic or "artificial" disk.

Cervical Spine Surgery

Cervical discectomy and fusion is a type of neck surgery that involves removing a damaged disc to relieve spinal cord or nerve root pressure and alleviate corresponding pain, weakness, numbness, and tingling

Spinal Cord Surgery

The procedure is performed through a surgical incision in the back.

Thalassemia Transplant

This tratment relies on high-dose chemotherapy to eliminate thalassemia-producing cells in the marrow and replaces them with the cells from a healthy donor, usually taken from a HLA match

Pediatric Bone Marrow

The same is repeated for children with a few alterations.

Autologous for Lymphomas - Bone Marrow

The patient’s own stem cells are used. They are collected several times in the weeks before he gets trated with a high-dose chemo and/or radiation dose and then are given back into the patient’s blood.

Multiple Myeloma - Bone Marrow

Treatments include medication, chemotherapy, corticosteroids, radiation or a stem-cell transplant on specific abnormalities within cancer cells that allow them to survive.

Chemotherapy

It is a type of treatment that includes a medication or combination of medications to treat cancer. The goal of chemo is to stop or slow the growth of cancer cells.

PET Scan

A positron emission tomography scan is an imaging test that allows your doctor to check for diseases in your body using radioactive tracers as special dyes.

Oral Cancer

Treatment includes surgery and radiation therapy. In some cases, chemotherapy may be required

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is cancer that develops from breast tissue. Its treatment depends on the stage of cancer. It may consist of chemotherapy, radiation, hormone therapy and surgery.

Cervical Cancer

Cervical cancer is a cancer arising from the cervix. It is due to the abnormal growth of cells that have the ability to invade or spread to other parts of the body. Treatments include surgery, radiation and chemotherapy.

Prostate Cancer

Some types of prostate cancer grow slowly. In some of these cases, monitoring is recommended. Other types are aggressive and require radiation, surgery, hormone therapy, chemotherapy or other treatments.

Angioplasty

It is a minimally invasive, endovascular procedure to widen narrowed or obstructed arteries or veins, typically to treat arterial atherosclerosis.

Heart Bypass (CABG)

It is a surgical procedure to restore normal blood flow to an obstructed coronary artery.

Angiography

It is a medical imaging technique used to visualize the inside, or lumen, of blood vessels and organs of the body, with particular interest in the arteries, veins and the heart chambers.

Aortic Valve Replacement

During this surgery, the damaged valve is removed and replaced with an artificial valve.

Mitral Valve Replacement

Mitral valve replacement is a cardiac surgical procedure in which a patient's diseased mitral valve is replaced by either a mechanical or bioprosthetic valve

Hole in the heart - ASD VSD

A heart defect due to an abnormal connection between the chambers of the heart. Most holes close on their own, although many may need surgery or a catheter-based procedure to close the hole.

Kidney Transplant

Kidney transplantation or renal transplantation is the organ transplant of a kidney into a patient who has end-stage renal disease.

Liver Transplant

Liver transplantation or hepatic transplantation is the replacement of a diseased liver with the healthy liver from another person.

Gastric Bypass

Gastric bypass surgery refers to a surgical procedure in which the stomach is divided into a small upper pouch and a much larger lower "remnant" pouch and then the small intestine is rearranged to connect to both.

Gastric Sleeve Surgery

It is a weight loss procedure that removes part of the stomach and restricts the amount of food that can be eaten.

Hair Transplant

Hair transplantation is a surgical technique that removes hair follicles from one part of the body, called the 'donor site', to a bald or balding part of the body known as the 'recipient site'

Brazilian Butt Lift

A cosmetic surgery that uses your own fat to enhance the size and shape of your buttocks.

Face Lift

A facelift, technically known as a rhytidectomy, is a type of cosmetic surgery procedure used to give a more youthful facial appearance.

Tummy Tuck

Tummy tuck surgery, also known as abdominoplasty, removes excess fat and skin and, in most cases, restores weakened or separated muscles creating an abdominal profile that is smoother and firmer.

IVF

IVF is a common infertility treatment. During the procedure, a fertility doctor takes the eggs from the ovaries using a small needle and fertilizes them with sperm in a specialized lab.

HCV
